Submarine Features

Our submarine is equipped with various hardware and software required for it to perform autonomously underwater, and to meet competition requirements. To perform tasks on its own without human intervention, it must be equipped with electrical systems inside of itself that can take in sensory information and go on to feed instructions to various mechanically controlled hardware.

Hardware

The submarine can sense information about it's environment through the use of hydrophones, cameras, and temperature, pressure, and depth sensors. Hydrophones utilize a form of sonar to assist in mapping the local environment alongside cameras. Pressure and depth sensors help tell the submarine where it is within water, to help orient itself and stay underwater.

To perform certain tasks, the submarine is also equipped with a torpedo launcher, a mechanical arm, a dropper, and propellers. These are all powered by electric motors.

Software

Multiple electrical circuits within the submarine feed information to the internal Raspberry Pi computer, which then will decide via our copyrighted and highly confidential software which instructions will be sent out. This interface between the Raspberry and the hardware happens thanks to the arduino onboard, which is capable of interpreting signals between all of the various circuits.

Development Cycle

We deemed it necessary to have a testing schedule during our development cycle to ensure an efficient use of time and resources. Between the beginning and end of the 2025 spring semester, we will perform [NUMBER] tests, in scope of what we expect our submarine to be capable of when the test is scheduled.

Test 1: Flooding

The most important thing is to keep all of the internal components of the submarine safe while it is underwater (we don't want another Titan submersible situation on our hands). This test will include submersion of the submarine and inspection for damage. It will also include human-controlled testing of the Arduino and propeller interface, to see if the submarine can move around how it should.

Test 2: Sonar Testing

In order for our submarine to navigate autonomously, it must be able to sense it's environment. This test will ensure that the hydrophones onboard and the Teledyne computer system is working properly.

Test 3: Autonomous Functionality

This test is just making sure that our submarine can execute software and hardware functions on its own, without any manual control.

Test 4: Obstacle Navigation

The final test will be an obstacle course, attempting to replicate an environment similar to what is expected at the competition. At this point, our submarine will be very close to competition ready.
